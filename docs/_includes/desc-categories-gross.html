{% if include.category == "AllCandidates" %}

  {% if include.length=="Head" or include.length=="Full" %}

  Debates and all-candidates meetings are interactive events where
  candidates running for a position answer rounds of questions.
  They allow you to compare candidates **side by
  side**, and are the **most effective way** to quickly compare 
  candidates.

  If you don't have time to watch an entire recording, watch 2-3 rounds
  of questions in the middle of the meeting. 
  Alternatively, you can **listen to the audio** of a debate
  like a podcast while walking or doing chores.

  {% endif %}
  {% if include.length=="Rest" or include.length=="Full" %}


  Questions to ask yourself:
  - Who attended the meeting? Who did not?
  - Which candidates answered the questions directly? Which ones avoided
    the question(s) or pivoted to talking points?
  - Which candidates demonstrated that they understand the complexities
    of the underlying issues? Which candidates offered overly-simplistic
    answers?

  {% endif %}

{% elsif include.category == "QuestionnairesEndorsements" %}

  {% if include.length=="Head" or include.length=="Full" %}

  Advocacy groups often publish questionnaires that allow you to
  compare candidates **side by side**. In addition some groups make
  explicit endorsements.  Pay **careful attention** to how
  questions are framed, and how this reflects the **positions and
  philosophies** of the advocacy groups. 

  {% endif %}
  {% if include.length=="Rest" or include.length=="Full" %}

  Questions to ask yourself:
  - Who sent the questionnaire? What were they advocating for?
  - To what extent do you agree with the positions of the group running
    the questionnaire?
  - Which candidates responded to the survey?
  - In what ways did the candidates spin their answers to try and appeal
    to the organization asking the questions?
  - Which candidates demonstrated a good understanding of the underlying
    issues?

  {% endif %}

{% elsif include.category == "EndorsementList" %}

  {% if include.length=="Head" or include.length=="Full" %}

  Some advocacy groups release endorsements of particular candidates.
  You can look at the advocacy group's **positions and philosophies**,
  and see whether they align with your own. 

  {% endif %}
  {% if include.length=="Rest" or include.length=="Full" %}

  Questions to ask yourself:
  - What does this group advocate for? Do you agree
    with their goals?
  - How did this group make their endorsements?
  - How did the group gather information about the candidates before
    making their endorsements?
  - How transparent was their selection process?

  Also see the **Opinions** section for discussions and articles
  that contain endorsements.

  {% endif %}


{% elsif include.category == "CandidateProfiles" %}

  {% if include.length=="Head" or include.length=="Full" %}

  Candidate profiles allow you to read responses from different
  candidates **side by side**.  They allow you to compare candidates
  based on their **backgrounds and experiences**. Often they will not
  tell you how candidates would address specific issues. 

  {% endif %}

{% elsif include.category == "Article" %}

  {% if include.length=="Head" or include.length=="Full" %}

  Articles can highlight issues of importance to the election and the
  electorate.  These items are (mostly) published by **established
  news organizations**.

  {% endif %}

{% elsif include.category == "Opinion" %}


  {% if include.length=="Head" or include.length=="Full" %}

  Opinions, blog posts and discussions **can be partisan**, but
  sometimes can be helpful in **bringing attention** to election issues.

  Sometimes discussions on forums like Reddit can give you a **range
  of opinions** about candidates and campaign issues.

  {% endif %}
  {% if include.length=="Rest" or include.length=="Full" %}

  Questions to ask yourself:

  - Can you identify where the writer(s) are coming from? Do you agree
    with their positions?
  - Do the posters offer evidence for their opinions? Is that evidence
    credible? Is it proportionate, or blown out of proportion?

  {% endif %}
